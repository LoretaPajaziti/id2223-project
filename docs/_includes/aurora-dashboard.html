Data Used for Prediction

The aurora forecast is based on a combination of geomagnetic, weather, and solar activity data. The model estimates the probability of aurora-favorable geomagnetic conditions for each of the next 5 days.

Geomagnetic Data (Daily)

Derived from global geomagnetic indices describing Earth’s magnetic activity. Each row corresponds to one day.

YYYY,MM,DD,days,days_m,BSR,dB, Kp1,Kp2,Kp3,Kp4,Kp5,Kp6,Kp7,Kp8, ap1,ap2,ap3,ap4,ap5,ap6,ap7,ap8, Ap,SN,F10.7obs,F10.7adj,D
  • Kp1–Kp8: 3-hour planetary Kp indices
  • Ap: Daily geomagnetic activity index (linear scale)
  • SN: Sunspot number
  • F10.7: Solar radio flux (proxy for solar activity)

Weather Data (Local, Sweden)

  • Cloud cover (%)
  • Precipitation (mm)
  • Sunshine duration

Weather conditions affect visibility but not aurora generation. They are included to contextualize viewing conditions.

Solar Wind Data (Training & Evaluation)

Solar wind parameters (e.g. velocity, magnetic field Bz, dynamic pressure) were also tested during model development. These variables help capture upstream solar-terrestrial interactions that drive geomagnetic storms.

Prediction categories (e.g. “Likely”, “Very likely”) are derived from model probabilities and do not represent direct observations.
Forecast based on geomagnetic, solar-wind, and weather data processed by a machine-learning model. Uncertainty increases with forecast horizon.
